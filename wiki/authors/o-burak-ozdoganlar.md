# O. Burak Ozdoganlar

## Bio Sketch
O. Burak Ozdoganlar is a professor of Mechanical Engineering at Carnegie Mellon University, with additional appointments in Biomedical Engineering and Materials Science and Engineering. His research focuses on precision manufacturing, micromachining, computational mechanics, and advanced materials processing. In the context of soft electronics, he has contributed manufacturing methodology for LM-based stretchable circuits — particularly UV laser micromachining (UVLM) for Cu wetting layer patterning and elastomeric circuit fabrication.

## Key Works
- *Adv. Mater.* (2014) — with Gozen, Tabatabai & Majidi; imprinting of LM microstructures in elastomers.
- *Adv. Mater. Interfaces* (2018) — with Ozutemiz, Wissman & Majidi; EGaIn–metal interfacing for microelectronics integration.

## Relevance to Bugra's Research
Ozdoganlar was Bugra's co-advisor at CMU alongside Carmel Majidi. His manufacturing expertise — particularly UV laser micromachining and precision fabrication of soft electronic circuits — directly shaped the fabrication methods developed in Bugra's doctoral research. Understanding Ozdoganlar's approach to precision manufacturing provides context for why certain fabrication choices were made (e.g., UVLM over shadow-mask deposition for finer resolution; Cu over Au for cost and process ubiquity).
