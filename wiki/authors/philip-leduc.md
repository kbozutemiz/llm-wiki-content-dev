# Philip R. LeDuc

## Bio Sketch
Philip R. LeDuc is a professor at Carnegie Mellon University with appointments spanning Mechanical Engineering, Biomedical Engineering, Computational Biology, and Biological Sciences. His research bridges mechanics, soft materials, and biology — including mechanobiology, biomechanics, microfluidics, and the mechanical design of soft/stretchable systems. In stretchable electronics, he has contributed fracture-mechanics-based strategies for embedding rigid components in soft substrates without delamination.

## Key Works
- *Adv. Mater.* (2016) — with Naserifar & Fedder; material gradients in stretchable substrates to suppress delamination of embedded silicon chips (ingested in this wiki).
- *Nat. Commun.* (2015) — with Egan, Sinko & Keten; mechanics of biological/biomimetic materials.
- Multiple works on PDMS microfabrication, microfluidics, and cell mechanics (e.g., *Lab Chip* 2011; *Biomaterials* 2015).

## Relevance to Bugra's Research
LeDuc is a CMU faculty member in the same mechanical engineering community as Bugra's doctoral work. His fracture-mechanics treatment of chip-in-elastomer delamination provides the quantitative foundation (energy release rate, interfacial adhesion energy) for understanding the soft-rigid interface failures that also limit liquid-metal circuits. The material-gradient strategy is directly relevant to the [Flexible Circuit Roadblocks](../projects/flexible-circuit-roadblocks/index.md) project's mechanical-reliability barrier — and complements the interconnect-focused work of Bugra's own research.
