# Carmel Majidi

## Bio Sketch
Carmel Majidi is a professor of Mechanical Engineering at Carnegie Mellon University (CMU), with additional appointments in Biomedical Engineering and Materials Science and Engineering. He directs the Soft Machines Lab, which focuses on soft-matter electronics, soft robotics, and multifunctional materials. He is one of the most active researchers in EGaIn-based stretchable electronics and is a central figure in the field of soft robotics and bio-integrated devices.

## Key Works
- *ACS Appl. Mater. Interfaces* (2014) — with Fassler; early LM-based deformation sensors.
- *Adv. Funct. Mater.* (2013) — with Kramer & Wood; selective deposition of EGaIn on Au patterns.
- *Adv. Sci.* (2017) — with Wissman & Dickey; electrowetting-on-liquid-metal.
- *Adv. Mater. Interfaces* (2018) — with Ozutemiz, Wissman & Ozdoganlar; EGaIn–metal interfacing for microelectronics integration (this wiki's first ingested source).

## Relevance to Bugra's Research
Majidi was Bugra's PhD advisor at CMU. The work in this wiki's first ingested source (Ozutemiz et al. 2018) was conducted in Majidi's Soft Machines Lab. Majidi's research program — scalable fabrication of LM-based stretchable circuits, microelectronics integration, and soft robotic systems — directly defines the technical context for Bugra's own work. His framing of the field's challenges (fabrication scalability, reliable microelectronics integration) is the lens through which the roadblocks project is understood.
