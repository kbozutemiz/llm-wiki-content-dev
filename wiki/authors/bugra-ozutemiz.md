# Kadri Bugra Ozutemiz

## Bio Sketch
Kadri Bugra Ozutemiz is a researcher in soft and stretchable electronics whose doctoral work at Carnegie Mellon University (Department of Mechanical Engineering) developed scalable fabrication and microelectronics-integration methods for EGaIn liquid-metal circuits. His research was conducted in collaboration with Carmel Majidi (Soft Machines Lab) and O. Burak Ozdoganlar, bridging soft-matter electronics and precision manufacturing. He is the originator and maintainer of this research wiki.

## Key Works
- *Adv. Mater. Interfaces* (2018) — first author, with Wissman, Ozdoganlar & Majidi; EGaIn–metal interfacing and HCl-vapor "soldering" for integrating surface-mount microelectronics with liquid-metal interconnects (this wiki's first ingested source).

## Relevance to Bugra's Research
This is Bugra's own work. The methods developed here — biphasic liquid-metal circuit fabrication, HCl-vapor soldering for reliable LM–IC interfaces, and self-alignment of components via liquid-metal surface tension — are foundational to the [Flexible Circuit Roadblocks](../projects/flexible-circuit-roadblocks/index.md) project. His first-author paper both demonstrates solutions to specific integration barriers and explicitly catalogs the remaining limitations (single-layer only, Al incompatibility, through-hole incompatibility, HCl sensitivity), making it a primary, high-reliability anchor for the project's analysis of commercialization barriers.
