# Gary K. Fedder

## Bio Sketch
Gary K. Fedder is a professor at Carnegie Mellon University with appointments in Electrical and Computer Engineering, Biomedical Engineering, Mechanical Engineering, and The Robotics Institute. He is a leading figure in MEMS (microelectromechanical systems) and CMOS-MEMS integration, known for techniques that combine micromechanical structures with standard foundry CMOS electronics. His perspective on integrating high-performance rigid microsystems informs strategies for embedding such electronics into stretchable substrates.

## Key Works
- *Adv. Mater.* (2016) — with Naserifar & LeDuc; material gradients for embedding silicon chips in stretchable substrates (ingested in this wiki).
- Extensive body of work on CMOS-MEMS integration, micromachined inertial and resonant sensors, and microsystem design.
- *Biomaterials* (2014) — with Kozai, Ozdoganlar et al.; neural probe / tissue-interface mechanics.

## Relevance to Bugra's Research
Fedder's expertise in CMOS-MEMS integration directly addresses one of the central challenges in the [Flexible Circuit Roadblocks](../projects/flexible-circuit-roadblocks/index.md) project: integrating high-performance, foundry-standard rigid electronics into soft, stretchable systems. His co-authored work on material gradients frames how rigid "island" components can be mechanically embedded in stretchable substrates — the complement to the liquid-metal interconnect ("bridge") problem that Bugra's own research addresses.
