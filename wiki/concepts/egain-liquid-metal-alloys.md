# EGaIn / Liquid Metal Alloys

## Definition
Gallium-based liquid metal (LM) alloys that are liquid at or near room temperature while maintaining metallic electrical conductivity. The two most commonly used are:

- **EGaIn** (eutectic gallium–indium): 75% Ga, 25% In by weight. Melting point: 15 °C.
- **Galinstan** (gallium–indium–tin): 68% Ga, 22% In, 10% Sn by weight. Melting point: −19 °C.

Key properties:
| Property | Value |
|---|---|
| Electrical conductivity | 3.4 × 10⁶ S m⁻¹ |
| Surface tension (EGaIn, bulk) | ≈435 mN m⁻¹ |
| Viscosity | ~2 mPa s |
| Melting point (EGaIn) | 15 °C |
| Melting point (Galinstan) | −19 °C |
| Toxicity | Low |
| Vapor pressure | Negligible |

EGaIn is liquid at room temperature and has metallic conductivity — a unique combination that enables it to function as an intrinsically stretchable conductor with no geometric constraints, unlike deterministic architectures or particle-filled composites.

## Related Concepts
- [Gallium Oxide (Ga₂O₃)](../concepts/gallium-oxide.md) — the native oxide skin that forms on all Ga-based LMs in air; governs wetting behavior and is both an enabler and a barrier.
- [Selective Wetting](../concepts/selective-wetting.md) — the mechanism by which EGaIn preferentially deposits on metal surfaces over elastomers.
- [Biphasic Stretchable Electronics](../concepts/biphasic-stretchable-electronics.md) — the primary fabrication architecture exploiting EGaIn's wetting properties.
- [Intermetallic Compound Formation](../concepts/intermetallic-compound-formation.md) — EGaIn alloys with Cu, Au, Pd, and Sn at interfaces; governs long-term stability of LM–metal contacts.
- [Electromechanical Coupling](../concepts/electromechanical-coupling.md) — how EGaIn resistance changes under applied strain.
- [Deterministic/Serpentine Architectures](../concepts/deterministic-serpentine-architectures.md) — a competing approach; EGaIn is contrasted with these throughout the literature.

## Advantages Over Alternative Stretchable Conductor Approaches
- **vs. percolating network conductors** (metallic particles, carbon allotropes in elastomers): EGaIn conductivity is ~3 orders of magnitude higher.
- **vs. conductive polymers**: EGaIn provides metallic conductivity and better electromechanical stability.
- **vs. deterministic/serpentine architectures**: EGaIn is stretchable at all length scales and in all directions without requiring specialized geometry or prestrained substrates.
