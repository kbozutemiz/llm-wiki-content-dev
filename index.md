## Concept Clusters

### Cluster A — Liquid Metal Electronics
*Materials, properties, and fabrication methods for gallium-based liquid metal circuits.*
**Core pages:** [EGaIn / Liquid Metal Alloys](wiki/concepts/egain-liquid-metal-alloys.md) · [Gallium Oxide (Ga₂O₃)](wiki/concepts/gallium-oxide.md) · [Selective Wetting](wiki/concepts/selective-wetting.md) · [Biphasic Stretchable Electronics](wiki/concepts/biphasic-stretchable-electronics.md) · [Intermetallic Compound Formation](wiki/concepts/intermetallic-compound-formation.md)
**Methods:** [HCl Vapor Soldering](wiki/methods/hcl-vapor-soldering.md) · [NaOH Treatment for Oxide Removal](wiki/methods/naoh-oxide-removal.md) · [Sputter Deposition on Elastomers](wiki/methods/sputter-deposition-elastomers.md)
**Key authors:** Carmel Majidi, O. Burak Ozdoganlar, Michael Dickey

- [EGaIn / Liquid Metal Alloys](wiki/concepts/egain-liquid-metal-alloys.md) — Ga-based LM alloys (EGaIn, Galinstan) with metallic conductivity at room temperature. Key thinker: Majidi, Dickey.
- [Gallium Oxide (Ga₂O₃)](wiki/concepts/gallium-oxide.md) — Self-passivating 0.5–2.5 nm oxide skin; governs all LM wetting behavior.
- [Selective Wetting](wiki/concepts/selective-wetting.md) — EGaIn preferentially wets metals (Cu, Au) over elastomers when oxide is removed.
- [Biphasic Stretchable Electronics](wiki/concepts/biphasic-stretchable-electronics.md) — Solid metal wetting layer + LM traces; core fabrication architecture. Key thinker: Majidi.
- [Intermetallic Compound Formation](wiki/concepts/intermetallic-compound-formation.md) — CuGa₂ and other intermetallics at LM–metal interfaces; governs adhesion and long-term stability.

### Cluster B — Stretchable Electronics Approaches (Comparative)
*The competing approaches to achieving stretchable electronic functionality.*
**Core pages:** [Stretchable Electronics](wiki/themes/stretchable-electronics.md) · [Deterministic/Serpentine Architectures](wiki/concepts/deterministic-serpentine-architectures.md) · [Percolating Network Conductors](wiki/concepts/percolating-network-conductors.md) · [Conductive Polymers](wiki/concepts/conductive-polymers.md)
**Key authors:** John A. Rogers (serpentine), Zhenan Bao (composites/polymers), Carmel Majidi (LM)

- [Stretchable Electronics](wiki/themes/stretchable-electronics.md) — Overview theme covering all approaches, challenges, and key researchers.
- [Deterministic/Serpentine Architectures](wiki/concepts/deterministic-serpentine-architectures.md) — Thin-film metals in serpentine/wavy geometries; directionally constrained. Key thinker: J.A. Rogers.
- [Percolating Network Conductors](wiki/concepts/percolating-network-conductors.md) — Conductive particles in elastomers; ~3 orders of magnitude below metallic conductivity.
- [Conductive Polymers](wiki/concepts/conductive-polymers.md) — Intrinsically conductive organic polymers; low conductivity but biocompatible. Key thinkers: Bao, Salleo.

### Cluster C — Soft-Rigid Integration and Mechanical Reliability
*Challenges at the interface between rigid ICs and soft substrates; mechanical, fracture, and fatigue behavior.*
**Core pages:** [Soft-Rigid Interface / Mechanical Mismatch](wiki/concepts/soft-rigid-interface-mechanical-mismatch.md) · [Graded Stiffness Substrate](wiki/concepts/graded-stiffness-substrate.md) · [Energy Release Rate](wiki/concepts/energy-release-rate.md) · [Interfacial Adhesion Energy](wiki/concepts/interfacial-adhesion-energy.md) · [Strain Shielding](wiki/concepts/strain-shielding.md) · [Cyclic Fatigue in Soft Electronics](wiki/concepts/cyclic-fatigue-soft-electronics.md)
**Key authors:** Carmel Majidi, O. Burak Ozdoganlar, Philip R. LeDuc, Gary K. Fedder

- [Soft-Rigid Interface / Mechanical Mismatch](wiki/concepts/soft-rigid-interface-mechanical-mismatch.md) — Stress concentrations at chip–elastomer boundaries; primary failure site in soft circuits.
- [Graded Stiffness Substrate](wiki/concepts/graded-stiffness-substrate.md) — Intermediate stiffness layer relocates strain off the rigid–soft interface; ~6× failure-strain gain. Key thinkers: LeDuc, Fedder.
- [Energy Release Rate](wiki/concepts/energy-release-rate.md) — Fracture-mechanics driving force for delamination; the design metric for soft-rigid interfaces.
- [Interfacial Adhesion Energy](wiki/concepts/interfacial-adhesion-energy.md) — Interface resistance to delamination; Si–PDMS is ~10⁴× weaker than PDMS–PDMS.
- [Strain Shielding](wiki/concepts/strain-shielding.md) — Diverting strain away from vulnerable interfaces via stiffer elements.
- [Electromechanical Coupling](wiki/concepts/electromechanical-coupling.md) — Resistance change under strain in LM traces; deviates from theory at high strains.
- [Cyclic Fatigue in Soft Electronics](wiki/concepts/cyclic-fatigue-soft-electronics.md) — Degradation under repeated loading; interface protection critical for durability.

### Cluster D — Soft Substrates and Fabrication
*The elastomer materials and processing methods underlying soft-electronic devices.*
**Core pages:** [PDMS](wiki/concepts/pdms.md) · [Island-Bridge Architecture](wiki/concepts/island-bridge-architecture.md)

**Key authors:** Philip R. LeDuc, Gary K. Fedder, O. Burak Ozdoganlar

- [Island-Bridge Architecture](wiki/concepts/island-bridge-architecture.md) — Rigid functional "islands" linked by stretchable "bridge" interconnects. Key thinker: J.A. Rogers.
